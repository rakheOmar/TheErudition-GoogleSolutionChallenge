# AMA Session Consolidated FAQs

## Solution Challenge 2026 - Build with AI (India)

### Event Overview
- **Organizer:** Google in partnership with Hack2Skill
- **Deadline:** 28th April 2026, 11:59 PM (IST)
- **Prize Pool:** ₹10 Lakhs (1st: ₹4L, 2nd: ₹3L, 3rd: ₹2L, Special Categories: ₹50K each)

---

## Submission Requirements

### What to Submit:
1. **Problem Statement** - Clearly define the issue and how your prototype solves it
2. **Live Prototype Link** - MVP demonstrating core functionality
3. **Project Deck (PPT)** - 10-15 pages, concise, detailing solution architecture and impact
4. **GitHub Repository** - Public repository with source code
5. **Demo Video** - Short video showcasing solution functionality

### Evaluation Criteria:
- **Technical Merit:** 40%
- **User Experience**
- **Alignment with Cause**
- **Innovation and Creativity**

### Evaluation Process:
1. Prototype Deck reviewed first
2. If PPT passes → Video Demo reviewed
3. If Demo passes → Prototype reviewed
4. If all pass → GitHub Repo reviewed

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: It's difficult to choose the domain and problem statement. How can I choose one?
**A:** Thoroughly go through all problem statements. Use Google tools like Gemini for research. Choose what resonates with you personally and what motivates you. Consider which problem affects the maximum number of people around you.

---

### Q2: Can we add extra slides in the PPT or should we stick to the template?
**A:** You can add content related to your prototype, but keep it concise. Recommended 10-15 pages. A couple extra pages are okay but don't exceed 2-3 pages over the template.

---

### Q3: What is the "live prototype link" for?
**A:** Demonstrate your working app/platform. Show core functionality - what exact problem it solves and how it works.

---

### Q4: Can we use a previous project for this hackathon?
**A:** Your idea can be something worked on before, but it must align with the provided problem statements. Ensure the problem statements are in sync with your project.

---

### Q5: Can the MVP link be an APK file for Android applications?
**A:** Yes, it can be. You can submit your Android app as an APK.

---

### Q6: Can I participate as a solo participant?
**A:** Yes, you can participate solo. It's not mandatory to participate in a team.

---

### Q7: If I submit on the 27th, will my project be neglected or judged last?
**A:** Submissions are open till 28th April. It will be evaluated like any other submission. You can submit multiple times before the deadline.

---

### Q8: Do judges value practical business impact and scalability as much as innovation?
**A:** Yes, it should be a balance of everything. The problem statements are practical and aligned with real-world challenges.

---

### Q9: Should the solution stay focused only on one industry (e.g., hospitality)?
**A:** Your prototype can be specific to an industry, but you can mention it can be deployed to other sectors as well. This demonstrates practical impact and scalability.

---

### Q10: I deployed my prototype on Vercel/Cloudflare/Netlify. Is it okay?
**A:** The submission asks about Google Cloud deployment specifically. If you deployed on Firebase (which is part of GCP), answer "Yes." For other platforms, reach out to the support team at solutions challenge support at ratehacktoskill.com for clarification.

---

### Q11: How can we get a prototype link for a mobile app?
**A:** You can submit an APK file. Include a demo video of the running Android app and the link to the APK.

---

### Q12: Is there access to previous winners' PPTs for reference?
**A:** No, that's not possible.

---

### Q13: Will judgment be based on PPT alone?
**A:** No, judgment is based on the entire prototype submission - the prototype link and demo video are key components.

---

### Q14: Is it compulsory to deploy on Google Cloud? Will GCP credits be provided?
**A:** Clarification on this will be sent via email by EOD. For specific queries, reach out to the support team.

---

### Q15: Should the demo video contain voice over?
**A:** Including voice over is recommended so evaluators understand what you're explaining. However, if the video is self-explanatory and you have detailed explanations in your PPT, it's not mandatory.

---

### Q16: My API tokens are getting exhausted quickly. How to resolve it?
**A:** Optimization may be needed. Write to the support team with more details about the usage.

---

### Q17: How to select problem statements? Can anyone guide?
**A:** Choose any one problem statement and start building. Each theme also has an "Open Innovation" category for broader real-world challenges under that theme.

---

### Q18: Does the demo video need to show real-life impact or just app functionality?
**A:** Show the working of the app. Real-life impact can be explained in detail in the PPT or deck.

---

### Q19: What is one common mistake teams make that reduces strong submission chances?
**A:** 
- Don't try to cover too many use cases
- Focus on solving one problem really well rather than 10 problems mediocrely
- Be concise in your deck - don't beat around the bush
- Stick to what the problem statement exactly requires

---

### Q20: Is frontend-only submission okay for live prototype?
**A:** Yes, the demo video will cover the frontend. You can submit the frontend as a live prototype.

---

### Q21: MVP link and prototype link - are they the same?
**A:** Yes, in this context, both mean the same. MVP (Minimal Viable Product) and prototype are used interchangeably here.

---

### Q22: Can we create our website using any platform other than Google?
**A:** You can use any platform that works for you. However, since this is a Google Solutions Challenge, using Google tools like Vertex AI or Google AI Studio is recommended.

---

### Q23: Is hosting free on Firebase or Google Cloud?
**A:** There are some free credits available which should be sufficient for prototyping. Try it out and write to support if facing issues.

---

### Q24: Is making a mobile app mandatory if I already have a web app?
**A:** No, if you have a web app, you can submit that as a prototype.

---

### Q25: My demo video is 10 minutes long. Will points be deducted?
**A:** If necessary to cover all functionalities, you can submit a longer video. However, since it's just a prototype, 2-3 minutes is recommended. Keep everything concise and within time limits.

---

### Q26: Solo or team - which is recommended?
**A:** For evaluators, it doesn't matter. If you can find a compatible team member, that helps, but solo participation is fully acceptable.

---

### Q27: How important is long-term scalability compared to immediate prototype functionality?
**A:** Immediate prototype functionality is more important. Long-term scalability can be mentioned and explained in the deck, but the current working prototype matters more.

---

### Q28: Is wide-level testing of prototype needed?
**A:** It's recommended to have friends/people around you test it, but wide-level testing depends on your prototype's scale.

---

### Q29: I'm making a VR game using Meta's Oculus Quest without Google services. Is it fine?
**A:** First, check if your VR game aligns with any problem statement. Since this is "Build with AI" by Google, using Google console and AI services is recommended. Reach out to support for eligibility clarification.

---

### Q30: One team member overrode commits. Is this okay?
**A:** As long as your code is still there, it's fine. You may have lost contribution history, but if you have a backup, you're good to go.

---

### Q31: Can I add two videos (project demo and field demo)?
**A:** The submission form allows one video. You can club both videos into one and submit.

---

### Q32: Can I add AI-generated clips in the demo video?
**A:** Yes, you can use AI video tools like Sora. You can show the problem people face and then demonstrate the solution, but ensure the actual prototype working is highlighted the most.

---

### Q33: Please extend the deadline - we're having practical exams.
**A:** The deadline has already been extended once. Unfortunately, it cannot be extended again. Submit at least a draft to ensure your place in the competition.

---

## Tips from the Team

1. **Start now** - You don't need to be perfect. Get your first draft in line with the core idea.
2. **Use the 6-step guide** - Beginner-friendly practical prompts to help you get started.
3. **Submit early** - You can submit multiple times before the deadline.
4. **Be concise** - Stick to the problem statement, don't try to solve too many things.
5. **Focus on one use case** - Better to ace one or two use cases than ten average ones.
